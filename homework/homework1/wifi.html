WiFi (or Wi-Fi) is known as Wireless Fidelity. A wireless networking technology that allows computers, mobile phones, tablets, video game consoles and other devices to communicate over a wireless signal. The device you own that has Wi-Fi can pick up a signal that connects it to the internet through the air. It's like a radio that can tune into a radio station signal over the airwaves. A WiFi signal is actuallya high-frequency radio signal.

  • They transmit at frequencies of 2.4 GHz or 5 GHz and useuse 802.11 networking standards.

  • 802.11a transmits at 5 GHz and can move up to 54 megabits of data per second.

  • 802.11b is the slowest and least expensive standard.

  • 802.11g transmits at 2.4 GHz like 802.11b, but it's a lot faster

  • 802.11n is the most widely available of the standards and is backward compatible with a, b and g.

  • 802.11ac is the newest standard
